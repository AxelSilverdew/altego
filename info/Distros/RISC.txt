**RISC OS** is a computer operating system originally designed by Acorn Computers Ltd in Cambridge, England in 1987, specifically to run on the ARM chipset. RISC OS is not a version of Linux, nor is it in any way related to Windows, and it has a number of unique features and aspects to its design. 
**Notable features**
- Runs on almost all ARM chips.
- Has it's own suite of applications.
- Is relatively tiny, runs well on SD cards.
- Extremely fast, but mostly used for hobbyist purposes.
